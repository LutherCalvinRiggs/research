# How to Grow on X (and Make Money From It): 7 Principles

**Source:** X post by @athcanft
**Saved:** 2026-06-17
**Tags:** business, writing, career, productivity, learning

> Author context: Started posting 3 months prior to writing. Revenue breakdown disclosed: X payouts ($4.7K), consulting ($10.5K), sponsorships ($2K), 3 B2B SaaS products ($8.5K). Niche: mobile apps / app marketing.

---

## TL;DR
Seven principles for growing an audience on X without "reply guy" tactics: provide genuine value, open with a numeric hook, post in every format (especially video), quote-tweet aggressively, recycle top performers via auto-retweet, monetize through multiple channels, and maintain authentic voice. The author attributes meaningful revenue (consulting, sponsorships, products) directly to consistent value-first posting.

## Key Concepts & Terms
- **The Hook**: The first line of any post — must convince the reader to keep reading within roughly one second of attention. Effective hooks in informational/educational niches use a clear numeric statistic ("I made $10K this month," "8% install-to-paid rate," "3 ways to..."). The number signals concrete value is coming.
- **Format diversity**: Posting the same knowledge repackaged across single tweets, threads, articles, and video. Raw, unedited video reportedly performs disproportionately well — the platform appears to favor it algorithmically.
- **Quote tweeting as a growth lever**: Quoting another popular post with a brief, relevant one-liner consistently drives outsized reach — described as working "nearly every time." A single quote tweet with a one-line reaction generated 39K likes and 2.8M views in the author's account.
- **Auto-retweeting bangers**: Re-surfacing your own best-performing posts on a schedule (the author used a self-built tool to auto-retweet one top post daily at a fixed time) to extract additional reach from already-proven content without ongoing manual effort.
- **Volume over uniform effort**: Not every post needs to be high-effort. The strategy is high posting frequency (5–10x/day) with a mix of polished and low-effort posts — virality is partly unpredictable, so volume increases the odds of a hit.

## The Seven Principles

### 1. Provide Value (the non-negotiable foundation)
Posts that teach something specific — a technique, a result, a process — earn engagement because they're useful to the reader. Posts with no hook, no story, and no result get scrolled past. The author's framing: pick your area of actual knowledge and teach it; the audience that wants that knowledge already exists on the platform.

### 2. The Hook
Same principle as any advertising headline — the first line has one job: earn the next three seconds of attention. Numeric specificity outperforms vague claims. "I made $10K this month" beats "I made some good money this month." The article's own title follows this rule deliberately.

### 3. Try Every Format
Post 5–10 times per day across single tweets, threads, articles, and video. Video specifically over-performs — raw, unedited footage is described as consistently doing well, attributed to platform-level algorithmic preference for video content. Mixed effort levels are fine; the strategy is volume plus variety, not uniform polish.

### 4. Quote Tweeting
A consistently reliable growth lever: quote-tweet a relevant popular post with a short, pointed one-liner. Reported to work "nearly every time" in the author's experience, with one example generating 2.8M views from a single one-line quote tweet.

### 5. Retweet Your Bangers
Re-share top-performing posts on a fixed schedule to extract additional reach. Large accounts reportedly do this routinely. The author automated it (self-built tool, daily fixed time) to remove the manual decision entirely.

### 6. Monetize
Multiple channels rather than one: platform payouts, consulting, sponsorships, and building products for the audience you've built. Framed as fair exchange — value provided to followers earns value back, across more than one revenue stream simultaneously.

### 7. Be Yourself
Authenticity is framed as increasingly valuable specifically because AI-generated and generic content is proliferating. The argument: performing a persona is unsustainable over time and eventually causes burnout; an authentic voice can't appeal to everyone but reliably attracts the right audience and is sustainable to maintain.

## Questions & Gaps
- The 3-month timeframe and disclosed revenue figures are self-reported with no independent verification. The principles may be sound even if exact figures aren't externally confirmed.
- "Video performs well because the platform pushes it" is an algorithmic claim without a cited source — platform algorithm behavior changes frequently and isn't typically publicly documented in detail.
- Quote tweeting "working nearly every time" — survivorship bias is a real risk here; failed quote tweets that didn't go viral aren't shown.
- No discussion of how niche-specific these tactics are. A numeric-hook strategy works well for finance/business/productivity niches; it's unclear how well it transfers to creative, artistic, or opinion-based content niches.
- No mention of negative externalities — quote-tweeting strategically for reach can read as opportunistic depending on execution; the line between "value exchange" and "farming engagement off others' content" isn't addressed.
